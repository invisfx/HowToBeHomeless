# Appendix B: National Resources {#appendix-b}

*Every program, hotline, and service referenced in this book --- organized by need.*

This directory lists national resources available to people experiencing homelessness or at risk of it. Most are free. All phone numbers are toll-free. Many operate 24/7. Save the numbers you need in your phone now, before you need them in a crisis.

## Emergency Hotlines

- **211** --- Dial 2-1-1 or visit 211.org. The single most important number in this book. Connects you to local shelters, food, utility assistance, healthcare, and social services. Available 24/7 in most areas. If you remember only one number, remember this one.
- **911** --- Life-threatening emergencies only. Police, fire, ambulance.
- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline** --- Call or text 988. Mental health crisis, suicidal thoughts, emotional distress. 24/7. Free. Confidential. Veterans press 1.
- **Crisis Text Line** --- Text HOME to 741741. Free 24/7 crisis counseling via text.
- **National Domestic Violence Hotline** --- 1-800-799-7233 (SAFE). 24/7. Safety planning, shelter referrals, legal help. Text START to 88788.
- **National Sexual Assault Hotline (RAINN)** --- 1-800-656-4673 (HOPE). 24/7. Free. Confidential. Also online chat at rainn.org.
- **National Human Trafficking Hotline** --- 1-888-373-7888. Text 233733.
- **SAMHSA Helpline** --- 1-800-662-4357 (HELP). Substance abuse and mental health treatment referrals. 24/7. Free. English and Spanish.

## Shelter and Housing

### Emergency Shelter

- **211** --- Your first call for shelter. Knows what beds are available tonight.
- **Salvation Army** --- salvationarmyusa.org --- Emergency shelters, transitional housing, food, and financial assistance in most major cities. Walk-in or call local branch.
- **Rescue Missions** --- agrm.org (Association of Gospel Rescue Missions) --- Over 300 rescue missions nationwide. Emergency shelter, meals, and recovery programs.
- **Catholic Charities** --- catholiccharitiesusa.org --- Shelter, rental assistance, food, and case management regardless of religion.
- **St. Vincent de Paul** --- svdpusa.org --- Emergency assistance with rent, utilities, food, and shelter referrals.

### HUD and Continuum of Care (CoC)

- **HUD Homeless Assistance** --- hud.gov/homeless --- Federal programs funding local shelters and housing. Find your local CoC at hudexchange.info/grantees/find-a-coc.
- **Continuum of Care (CoC)** --- Your local CoC coordinates all homeless services in your area: shelters, transitional housing, permanent supportive housing, rapid rehousing. Contact through 211 or your local shelter.
- **Coordinated Entry** --- The system that assesses your needs (VI-SPDAT) and connects you to housing programs. Access through 211, shelters, or outreach workers.

### Housing Programs

- **Section 8 / Housing Choice Vouchers** --- Contact your local Public Housing Authority (PHA). Find yours at hud.gov/program_offices/public_indian_housing/pha/contacts. Waitlists are long (2--10 years) but get on them now.
- **Rapid Rehousing** --- Short-term rental assistance (3--12 months). Access through CoC coordinated entry.
- **Permanent Supportive Housing** --- Long-term housing with case management for chronically homeless and disabled. Access through CoC.
- **Housing First programs** --- No preconditions (sobriety, employment) for housing. Highest success rate. Ask your caseworker.
- **Bridge Housing** --- Temporary housing while waiting for permanent placement. Typically 30--90 days. Access through shelters and CoC.

### Rental and Deposit Assistance

- **Salvation Army** --- Grants up to $1,500 for rent and deposits.
- **Catholic Charities** --- Emergency rent assistance.
- **Community Action Agencies** --- communityactionpartnership.com --- Local agencies providing rent, utility, and deposit assistance. Find yours by zip code.
- **Modest Needs** --- modestneeds.org --- Grants for people in temporary crisis. They pay your landlord directly.
- **Local churches** --- Many have discretionary funds. Call and ask.

## Food

- **Feeding America** --- feedingamerica.org/find-your-local-foodbank --- Largest hunger relief network. 200+ food banks, 60,000 pantries nationwide.
- **SNAP (Food Stamps)** --- Apply at your state DHS or fns.usda.gov/snap. No address required. Expedited if under $150 in assets.
- **WIC** --- fns.usda.gov/wic --- Women, Infants, and Children nutrition program. Pregnant women, new mothers, children under 5.
- **foodpantries.org** --- Searchable database of food pantries by zip code.
- **Too Good To Go** --- toogoodtogo.com --- Surplus restaurant food, $3--$5 per bag.
- **Community fridges** --- Search "community fridge [your city]" on social media. Free, 24/7, no ID.

## Government Benefits

- **SNAP** --- fns.usda.gov/snap --- $200--$300/month for food. Apply at state DHS.
- **Medicaid** --- healthcare.gov or 1-800-318-2596 --- Free health insurance in 40 expansion states.
- **Unemployment Insurance** --- File at your state workforce website. Day of layoff.
- **TANF** --- Temporary Assistance for Needy Families. Cash assistance for families with children. Apply at state DHS.
- **SSI / SSDI** --- ssa.gov or 1-800-772-1213 --- Disability benefits. SSI for limited work history, SSDI for sufficient work credits.
- **General Assistance (GA)** --- State/county cash assistance for individuals without children. Varies widely. Contact state DHS.
- **LIHEAP** --- liheapch.acf.hhs.gov --- Low Income Home Energy Assistance. Helps pay utility bills.
- **Lifeline Phone Program** --- lifelinesupport.org --- $9.25/month discount or free phone (Assurance Wireless, SafeLink).
- **Benefits screener** --- findhelp.org --- Enter your zip code and situation, see every program you qualify for.

## Healthcare

- **FQHCs (Community Health Centers)** --- findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov --- Sliding scale fees. Cannot turn you away.
- **Free clinics** --- freeclinics.com --- Directory of free medical clinics.
- **Planned Parenthood** --- plannedparenthood.org --- Reproductive and primary care. Sliding scale.
- **Hill-Burton free care** --- 1-800-638-0742 --- Hospitals with obligation to provide free/reduced care.
- **Dental schools** --- ada.org/education --- 50--80% cheaper dental care from supervised students.
- **GoodRx** --- goodrx.com --- Free app, saves 30--80% on prescriptions.
- **NeedyMeds** --- needymeds.org --- Patient assistance programs, drug discount cards.
- **EMTALA** --- ERs must treat you regardless of ability to pay. Report violations: 1-800-633-4227.

## Veterans

- **VA Homeless Veteran Hotline** --- 1-877-424-3838 (4AID-VET). Call for immediate help. 24/7.
- **Veterans Crisis Line** --- Call 988, press 1. Or text 838255.
- **HUD-VASH** --- Section 8 voucher + VA case management. The fastest path to housing for veterans. Contact through VA or 211.
- **SSVF (Supportive Services for Veteran Families)** --- Rapid rehousing grants for veterans. Apply through VA or local SSVF provider.
- **GPD (Grant and Per Diem)** --- Transitional housing for veterans. Up to 24 months.
- **Stand Down Events** --- Annual events in major cities providing free services (food, clothing, medical, legal, haircuts, benefits enrollment). Search "Stand Down [your city]."
- **VA Healthcare** --- va.gov/health-care/apply --- No income requirement for combat veterans. Enroll even if you have other insurance.

## Families with Children

- **McKinney-Vento School Liaison** --- Call your school district. Every district must have one. They ensure your children stay enrolled, get transportation, and receive services. Federal law (42 U.S.C. ss 11431).
- **Head Start** --- eclkc.ohs.acf.hhs.gov --- Free preschool for low-income children ages 3--5.
- **WIC** --- fns.usda.gov/wic --- Nutrition for pregnant women and children under 5.
- **TANF** --- Cash assistance for families with minor children. Apply at state DHS.
- **Family shelters** --- Call 211. Separate from general population shelters. Often have longer stays and more services.
- **CPS fact:** Homelessness alone is NOT child neglect. No state defines it as such. If anyone threatens CPS action solely because you are homeless, they are wrong.

## Youth (Under 25)

- **National Runaway Safeline** --- 1-800-786-2929 (RUNAWAY). 24/7. Also chat at 1800runaway.org. For youth up to 21.
- **Trevor Project** --- 1-866-488-7386. LGBTQ+ youth crisis and suicide prevention. 24/7. Text START to 678-678.
- **True Colors United** --- truecolorsunited.org --- LGBTQ+ youth homelessness resources.
- **Covenant House** --- covenanthouse.org --- Shelter and services for youth 18--24 in major cities.
- **Job Corps** --- jobcorps.gov --- Free education and job training for ages 16--24. Includes housing, meals, healthcare, and a living allowance.
- **AmeriCorps** --- americorps.gov --- Service programs that provide living stipend, housing, and education awards. Ages 17+.

## Women

- **National DV Hotline** --- 1-800-799-7233. Safety planning and shelter referrals. 24/7.
- **Women-only shelters** --- Call 211 for locations in your area.
- **Planned Parenthood** --- Reproductive health, contraception, prenatal care. Sliding scale.
- **Dress for Success** --- dressforsuccess.org --- Free professional clothing and career support for women.

## Legal Help

- **Legal Aid** --- lawhelp.org --- Free civil legal help based on income. Eviction defense, benefits appeals, ID replacement, discrimination.
- **ACLU** --- aclu.org --- Civil rights violations, unconstitutional sweeps, free speech cases.
- **National Homelessness Law Center** --- homelesslaw.org --- Know-your-rights resources, policy advocacy.
- **Public defenders** --- Free attorney if charged with a crime. Right guaranteed by Gideon v. Wainwright (1963).
- **Homeless courts** --- Available in some cities. Resolve warrants and fines without jail. Ask your caseworker or call 211.
- **Law school clinics** --- Free legal services from supervised law students. Call your nearest law school.

## Employment

- **American Job Centers** --- careeronestop.org --- Free job search, resume help, training, and career counseling. 2,400+ locations.
- **Goodwill** --- goodwill.org --- Job training, placement, and career services. Also free/cheap work clothes.
- **Dress for Success** --- dressforsuccess.org --- Free professional clothing for women.
- **PeopleReady / JobStack** --- Day labor app. Work today, get paid today.
- **Job Corps** --- jobcorps.gov --- Free job training + housing for ages 16--24.
- **VITA tax prep** --- irs.gov/vita --- Free tax filing. You may be owed a refund + EITC.

## Financial

- **Chime** --- chime.com --- No-fee bank account. No minimum balance. Free debit card.
- **Cash App** --- Free debit card, direct deposit, $cashtag for receiving donations.
- **GoFundMe** --- gofundme.com --- Crowdfunding for personal emergencies.
- **Modest Needs** --- modestneeds.org --- Grants for temporary financial crises.
- **IRS Free File** --- irs.gov/freefile --- Free tax filing if income under $84,000.
- **GoodRx** --- goodrx.com --- Prescription discounts, 30--80% off.

## Other Essential Services

- **USPS General Delivery** --- Free mailing address at any post office. Ask at the counter.
- **Voter registration** --- vote.org --- You can register without a traditional address.
- **FEMA disaster assistance** --- disasterassistance.gov or 1-800-621-3362. Available without permanent address.
- **Narcan / naloxone** --- Available OTC at most pharmacies. Free at many harm reduction programs. Search nextdistro.org for free mail delivery.
- **NAMI (mental health)** --- 1-800-950-6264 --- Mental health support, peer groups, family resources.
- **AA / NA meetings** --- aa.org / na.org --- Free. No requirements. Meetings everywhere, daily.
