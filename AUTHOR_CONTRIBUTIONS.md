# Author Contributions — Doug Addy

This document records the creative decisions, editorial direction, and original contributions made by the author throughout the development of *No Quarter: A Survival Manual*.

---

## Conception and Vision

- Conceived the entire book project — a practical survival guide for people facing homelessness due to mass layoffs
- Defined the target audience: working people who suddenly lose everything, not chronic homeless
- Set the core philosophy: practical, no fluff, real resources, treat readers as capable adults
- Directed the tone: field manual, not self-help. Street soldiers, not victims

## Title and Branding

- Rejected 21+ title candidates based on feel and fit
- Selected "No Quarter" as the final title — identifying the triple meaning (no money, no housing, no mercy)
- Connected the title to Led Zeppelin's "No Quarter" — "Close the door, turn out the light, no they won't be home tonight"
- Created the tagline: "Street soldiers fighting for their lives"
- Selected the subtitle: "A Survival Manual" — rejecting "A Field Manual for Starting from Nothing" as inaccurate ("you're not starting from nothing, you had a life")
- Named Chapter 1 "Damage Control" over alternatives including Prevention, Firebreak, The Runway, Brace for Impact — reasoning that damage control is a comprehensive strategy, not a single tool

## Structure and Organization

- Defined the 11-part structure covering the full arc from pre-homelessness through recovery
- Directed chapter ordering and logical flow
- Decided to move legal rights and national resources to the back as reference material (not meant to be read through)
- Requested a prevention/damage control chapter before triage — "a plan to use the resources available before you become homeless"
- Insisted on notes pages at the end of every chapter for reader use
- Requested action checklists in key chapters
- Directed the addition of a "best places to be homeless" section in Part X

## Content Direction and Original Knowledge

- **Trucker's hitch knot**: Directed addition for tarp tensioning — "it's real important to get that rope tight"
- **Duct tape as fire starter**: Contributed the knowledge that duct tape burns hot and slow with a cigarette lighter
- **Cigarette lighters**: Directed addition to gear list as essential carry items
- **Iodine multi-use**: Identified iodine's versatility (water purification + wound care + fire starting)
- **LifeStraw capacity**: Corrected from 10,000 to 1,000 gallons
- **Sawyer Squeeze price**: Corrected to current Amazon price ($44, not $25)
- **Beef jerky pricing**: Flagged that prices have increased significantly
- **Backpack rain cover**: Recommended for dual purpose — waterproofing and concealment ("so it looks less like a homeless person's bag")
- **Pen and paper saves phone battery**: Contributed this practical insight
- **Resume and ID photos**: Directed addition to the digitize documents section
- **Knot IQ app**: Recommended as a learning resource
- **Iodine + aluminum thermite idea**: Proposed, then agreed it was too dangerous — demonstrating editorial judgment on safety boundaries
- **Fresnel lens**: Corrected "included with this book" language since book format changed
- **Dollar Tree vs Walmart**: Identified that Walmart is more practical (more locations, restrooms, longer hours, phone charging)
- **Deodorant pricing**: Caught that $1 deodorant isn't realistic — "you can't get deodorant for $1"
- **Practice rough sleeping**: Proposed the "dry run" concept — practice sleeping outside before you need to, like military field exercises
- **Begging psychology**: Directed addition of Cialdini's influence principles applied to panhandling (reciprocity, social proof, foot-in-the-door, etc.)
- **De-escalation psychology**: Requested psychological techniques for conflict avoidance
- **Government free phone program**: Identified Lifeline program and California-specific benefits
- **Online fundraising**: Directed addition of GoFundMe, Cash App, and mutual aid network content
- **Retirement savings**: Directed deep research into 401k withdrawal, Roth IRA contributions, hardship withdrawals
- **Exit money strategy**: Asked "how much money can you have and not pay rent?" — driving the asset limits and benefits qualification content
- **Hydration and electrolytes**: Directed expansion of this section
- **Creative survival strategies**: Directed inclusion of gray-area tactics — "let's not shy away from not legal things as long as it doesn't hurt anyone. Going to jail for sleeping on a bench isn't a bad result, just note that it is illegal"
- **Linktree/distribution**: Directed exploration of sharing and donation platforms

## Editorial Decisions

- Rejected lorem ipsum placeholder text — "we need real proven content"
- Demanded practical content over fluff — "this needs to be thick with practical content no fluff"
- Directed font size increase from 8.5pt to 11pt for readability
- Directed wider page format for compact physical book — "this is for homeless so it needs to be compact"
- Requested color-coding of all web URLs and phone numbers for quick reference
- Requested footnoted cross-references with page numbers
- Identified and directed fixing of ch01 redundancy — "Triage is very redundant partly due to its organization"
- Directed single-column layout for list-heavy pages — "put these pages with long lists on single lines not split down the middle"
- Caught page number positioning issues — "the html page number is not at the bottom of some pages"
- Caught page height inconsistencies — "the page lengths are different, the actual heights change, this doesn't happen in a real book"
- Directed workflow change from HTML to Markdown for better editing
- Selected Obsidian as the editing tool
- Directed all price verification across the entire book
- Made the repository private, then public again for editor access
- Directed copyright and legal disclaimer inclusion
