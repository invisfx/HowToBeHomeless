♟ The Game: Camp Politics — Repeated Games and Cooperation

An encampment is a repeated game: the same players interact daily, which changes optimal strategy. In a one-shot interaction, defection (theft, freeloading) may pay. In a repeated game, tit-for-tat dominates — cooperate first, then mirror what others do. Camps that survive develop informal enforcement: shared watch schedules, contribution norms, expulsion of bad actors. When joining a camp, your first move should be cooperation (offer to watch someone's stuff, share a resource). This establishes reciprocity. But also: be willing to leave camps where defection is the norm. You cannot fix a broken equilibrium alone.
